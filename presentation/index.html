On-chain digital legacy. Heartbeat while you can — the chain settles when you can't.

Hi, I'm Diego. In the next 5 minutes I'll show you Estate Protocol — a Polkadot parachain that lets you register an on-chain will that auto-executes if you stop sending heartbeats. I'll walk you through the problem, the architecture across three chains, and close with a quick demo.

the problem

Keys die with you ×_×

🔑 + 💀 = 🔒 forever

Crypto has a quiet inheritance problem. Private keys die with their owner. There's no probate, no executor, no legal framework that can reach cold storage. Multisigs and custodial exchanges exist as workarounds, but they either require you to pre-select heirs today and give them a key right now, or they re-introduce the counterparty risk Web3 was built to remove. We need an on-chain, non-custodial answer.

the solution

A programmable will.

Silence is the trigger. Heartbeats keep it alive.

Register a will — a typed list of actions, each naming a beneficiary. You heartbeat periodically; every heartbeat reschedules the expiry. If you go silent past the interval, the runtime's scheduler deterministically fires the will. Each beneficiary gets a soulbound NFT — a permanent on-chain receipt of inheritance. You can cancel at any time.

architecture

Three chains. One relay.

Estate Protocol is our parachain — it holds the will state and runs the scheduler. People Chain is the Polkadot system parachain that hosts pallet-identity; we consult it to verify every heir has a real identity. Asset Hub is where the assets actually live. When a will fires, Estate sends an XCM message to Asset Hub that dispatches the inheritance as the owner. The heir also gets a soulbound NFT minted back on Estate.

xcm

Assets never leave Asset Hub.

One-time proxy grant. Estate dispatches as you.

The key design decision: Estate Protocol never custodies your assets. They stay on Asset Hub. What you do once is grant our sovereign account a ProxyType::Any over your Asset Hub account — the same primitive every wallet already uses. When a will executes, we send an XCM Transact to Asset Hub wrapped in Proxy.proxy so it dispatches as you. If anything ever went wrong with Estate, you revoke the proxy and your assets are untouched.

Three fees keep the protocol self-sustaining. A longevity fee per heartbeat, an execution fee reserved at create and charged when the will fires, and a trigger reward paid out of that same execution fee if someone has to manually fire an overdue will. All in the native token — no new asset. Everything flows into a SplitTwoWays router: 30% burns, 70% goes to a PalletId-derived treasury. The trigger reward is FLAT-capped — a million-ROC will and a one-ROC will pay the same maximum bounty, so big wills are never coercion targets, and the payout is always bounded by what the owner actually paid in.

// Scheduler-first. If congestion makes it miss the block,
// *anyone* can fire an overdue will and collect a bounty
// that scales with lateness — capped at one flat fee.
pub fn trigger(origin, id: WillId) -> DispatchResult {
    let who  = ensure_signed(origin)?;
    let will = Wills::<T>::get(id).ok_or(Error::WillNotFound)?;
    ensure!(will.active(), Error::AlreadyExecuted);
    ensure!(now > will.expiry_block, Error::TooEarlyToTrigger);
    let reward = TriggerRewardPerBlock::get()
        .saturating_mul(blocks_overdue.into())
        .min(TriggerRewardCap::get());
    Self::do_execute(id, will, Some((who, reward)))
}
Seven quick steps through one Config impl. One: Scheduler is pallet-scheduler — it's what actually fires execute_will at expiry_block + 1; create_will schedules the task, heartbeat reschedules it. Two: IdentityCheck is the hook that verifies every heir has a real identity on People Chain. Three: the three fee parameters — FeeRouter, FeePerBlock for the longevity fee, and the flat per-bequest fee. Four: the two reward parameters for the manual trigger — per-block rate and a hard cap. Five: FeeRouter is just SplitTwoWays from frame_support — 30 parts burn, 70 parts into the treasury. Six: feeless_if lets the owner heartbeat for zero tx fee, by the one predicate shown. Seven: trigger is the manual fallback — if the scheduler misses the block, any account can fire an overdue will and collect a bounty that scales with lateness but is capped at one flat fee. The cap is the safety rail: a one-ROC will and a one-million-ROC will pay the same maximum bounty, so large wills are never coercion targets. Every knob is a runtime constant, governance-tunable without touching the pallet.

the xcm

Dispatch as the owner. No custody.

pub enum Bequest<T: Config> {
    Transfer      { dest: T::AccountId, amount: T::Balance },
    TransferAll   { dest: T::AccountId },
    Proxy         { delegate: T::AccountId },
    MultisigProxy {
        delegates: BoundedVec<T::AccountId, MaxMultisigDelegates>,
        threshold: u16,
    },
}

// Build the XCM that runs on Asset Hub as the owner.
fn build_ah_proxy_xcm(owner: &AccountId, inner: Vec<u8>) -> Xcm<()> {
    let fees: Asset = (Location::parent(), 100 * ROC).into();
    let proxy_call = ah_wrap_proxy(owner, inner);  // Proxy.proxy(owner, _, call)
    Xcm(vec![
        WithdrawAsset(fees.clone().into()),
        BuyExecution { fees, weight_limit: WeightLimit::Unlimited },
        Transact {
            origin_kind: OriginKind::SovereignAccount,
            call: proxy_call.into(),
            fallback_max_weight: None,
        },
    ])
}
Two steps. One: the Bequest enum — the typed menu of what a will can do. A fixed-amount transfer, sweep all, hand a proxy to a single delegate, or to a multisig threshold of delegates. Two: when a will fires, build_ah_proxy_xcm assembles three XCM instructions. WithdrawAsset and BuyExecution pay for dispatch on Asset Hub out of our sovereign's AH balance. Transact carries a Proxy.proxy(owner, _, call) wrap — that's the critical piece. Asset Hub's proxy pallet dispatches the inner call as the owner, not as our parachain. Net effect: the owner's assets move, signed by the owner, without Estate ever touching them. If anything ever went wrong with Estate, the owner revokes the proxy and their assets are untouched.
